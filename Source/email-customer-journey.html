4 emails ~5 days

Welcome flow
Post-purchase onboarding — starts Day 28 after order
1–3 emails 4 days
Trigger: 28 days after the customer places an order (no-risk period ends). Customer is classified into 1 of 4 domains based on their last order.
⛔Stop condition: Customer opens any email in the flow.
⚠️Recommendation: "Stop on open" is unreliable since Apple Mail Privacy Protection auto-triggers ~49% of opens. Change to "stop on click or purchase" for more accurate engagement signal.
🔁
6-Month Refreshment
Product lifecycle reminder — BraGoddess only
1 email per cycle repeats
Trigger: 6 months after the customer's last bra or panties purchase. Cycle repeats — another email is queued 6 months after every new bra/panty order.
💡Recommendation: Single email rarely converts. Consider expanding to 2–3 emails: reminder → +5d social proof → +5d urgency + offer. Stop if any purchase happens.
🔄
Winback flow
Re-engagement after 60 days inactive — 7 days between each email, stops on open
1–4 emails up to 21 days
Trigger: 60 days of no email opens AND no purchases. Each next email only sends if the previous one was NOT opened. As soon as the customer opens any email, they return to the active customer database and the flow stops.
⛔Stop condition: Customer opens any email → returns to active customer database, flow ends immediately.
✓Smart design: Email-by-email open check is the right pattern. It avoids over-emailing customers who re-engage early and gives full 4-email runway to those who don't.
🎉
Anniversary flow
Loyalty celebration — 1 email per milestone, counted from very first order
5 emails over 5 years
Trigger: Exactly 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 years from the customer's very first order date. Sent from the domain matching their most recent order at the time of each milestone.
⚠️Open question: What happens after year 5? Continue annually, or graduate to a dedicated VIP path?
💡Recommendation: Tie escalating rewards to each milestone (Y1 = 10% off, Y2 = free shipping, Y3 = early access, Y4 = VIP coupon, Y5 = mystery gift) to turn these into a loyalty engine.

Future flows — proposed timing

Each future flow with a suggested email count, timing, and trigger logic. These have been mapped into the master timeline above.

Browse Abandon — proposed
Pre-cart intent capture
2 emails future
Trigger: Customer views a product page for 1+ minute without adding to cart. Catches intent earlier than Cart Abandon.
⛔Stop condition: Customer adds to cart (→ Cart Abandon flow takes over) OR completes purchase.
🎂
Birthday / Birth Month — proposed
Annual celebration
1 email / year future
Trigger: Customer's birthday OR 1st day of their birth month. Repeats every year.
Suggested timing: Send on the 1st of the birth month (gives them the full month to redeem the gift/coupon) — less disruptive than birthday-day surprises.
💡Recommendation: Pair with a 30-day coupon code so customers feel they have time to use the offer. Send from the customer's last-order domain like other flows.
⚠️Data requirement: Need to capture birthday data at signup or via profile update. Most customers don't share this — expect 30–50% coverage even with prompts.
💎
Loyalty Milestones — proposed
Spending or order-count thresholds
~4 emails future
Trigger: Customer crosses a spend or purchase milestone.
Suggested thresholds:
• Bronze: $100 spent OR 3rd order
• Silver: $250 spent OR 5th order
• Gold: $500 spent OR 10th order
• Platinum: $1,000+ spent OR 20th order

1 email per milestone, sent immediately when threshold is crossed. Includes the new status badge + tier-specific reward.
💡Recommendation: Make rewards meaningful and visible in subsequent emails (e.g., "Gold member: 15% off everything" in the email footer for all subsequent sends). The tier should feel like ongoing status, not just a one-time email.
🏆
Monthly Top 100 — proposed
Highest spenders each month
1 email / month future
Trigger: Customer ranks in top 100 spenders for the calendar month.
Suggested timing: Calculate at end of month, send on 1st–3rd of the new month. Includes a coupon or free gift redeemable within 30 days.
⚠️Volume consideration: If a customer stays in the top 100 every month, they could receive 12 of these per year — on top of every other flow. Define if they should also still receive the Loyalty milestone emails or if Top 100 supersedes those.

Analysis & recommendations

Priority-ranked observations about timing, logical progression, and overlap between flows.

Critical
Welcome flow "stop on open" is unreliable Apple Mail Privacy Protection auto-triggers opens for ~49% of subscribers. Many customers never receive emails 2 or 3 due to phantom opens, not real engagement. Action: change stop condition to "stop on click or purchase."
Critical
Define global per-customer weekly email cap before launching future flows With all current + future flows, a top-100 BraGoddess customer could receive 7–10 emails per week. Action: set a hard cap (e.g., max 5 emails/customer/week) and define which flow wins when multiple trigger.
Medium

Medium

Medium
SantaFare off-season domain assignment Winback/Anniversary emails sent from SantaFare in March–October promote nothing. Action: temporarily reassign SantaFare-last-order customers to their second-most-recent domain during off-season.
Medium

Low
Consider a lightweight Day 3 welcome email 28 days is a long wait — peak engagement is days 0–7 post-purchase. A brief Day 3 welcome (outside the main flow) would capture early enthusiasm without interfering with the no-risk return logic.
Low

Low
