## How to Read This Report

### The 4 Metrics (in order of importance)

| Metric | What It Measures | Caveats |
|---|---|---|
| **CBH/Delivered** | Revenue efficiency — gross profit per email sent. The closest proxy to profitability. | Most important. A send can have great open rates and still be unprofitable. |
| **Access/Open** | Click-to-open rate among people who opened. MPP-immune — only real human opens click through. | True engagement signal. Not distorted by Apple Mail Privacy Protection. |
| **PO/View** | Purchase orders per product page view. Conversion of browsers to buyers. | Measures offer-product fit. Low here = wrong product or wrong audience. |
| **Open/Delivered** | Share of list that opened. | **Inflated by Apple MPP** — passive opens from Apple Mail users (~49% of subscribers) are counted. Treat with skepticism. |

### Critical Context

**Apple MPP inflation:** Apple Mail pre-fetches open pixels, so any subscriber using Apple Mail records an "open" regardless of whether they saw the email. For the core list, Open/Delivered runs 0.40–0.55 on good sends. The +Yahoo segment sends show 0.27–0.30 — because Yahoo users aren't MPP-affected, this window exposes the real underlying open rate (~28%).

**+Yahoo segment sends:** An extra blast to an inactive/Yahoo-heavy list appended to the main send. These consistently suppress CBH/Delivered by 40–60% compared to core-list-only sends. The delivered count inflates while revenue doesn't follow proportionally.

**CBH defined:** Contribution to Business Health = gross profit proxy. GentsLux AOV ($55–82) is roughly 2× BraGoddess ($33–45), which explains why GentsLux routinely posts higher absolute CBH/Del even with similar engagement metrics.

**RFM segments:** A = high-engagement recent buyers, B = solid engagement, C = moderate, D = lower engagement.

### BraGoddess Key Patterns

- **Birthday emails never fail.** Across all 6 months, Happy Birthday sends rank in the top 3 for Access/Open in 5 of 6 months. They are the most reliable engagement driver in the portfolio.

## Cross-Brand Insights

### 3 Things to DO MORE OF

**1. Personalized trigger emails: Birthday, Back in Stock, Anniversary**
- Birthday emails rank in the top 3 for Access/Open in 20 of 24 brand-months across the dataset.
- BraGoddess Birthday sends average 0.0080 CBH/Del vs. 0.0055 for generic sends — a 45% premium.
- SantaFare's only March send above 0.0010 was a birthday trigger (0.0050). It was the last surviving revenue signal before the brand's collapse.
- Back in Stock (BraGoddess Jan 14: 0.0065, LuxFitting) outperforms generic sends by providing a specific, customer-relevant reason to act — a textbook Jobs to Be Done trigger.

**2. Segmented sends to the high-value core list — without +Yahoo dilution**
- GentsLux April makes the case definitively: 155K concentrated list → 0.0276 CBH/Del. Same brand, same week, 385K list → 0.0068 CBH/Del. The larger list is 4× less profitable per email sent.
- +Yahoo sends suppress CBH/Del by 40–60% across every brand, every month, without exception in the dataset.
- The revenue is in the top 20% of the list. Sending to the full list inflates delivered count, inflates suppression/optout risk, and dilutes the metrics that actually matter.

**3. Themed, story-driven content with a clear seasonal or occasion hook**
- BraGoddess "Winter 2025" (26 Nov: 0.0108), GentsLux "Winter 2025" (19 Nov: 0.0194 Access/Open — highest in entire dataset), LuxFitting "Christmas" (12 Dec: 0.0088), SantaFare "Black Friday" (20 Nov: 0.0132) — all seasonal-hook sends are top performers within their respective months.
- The pattern holds even mid-season: BraGoddess "Valentine's D" (13 Feb: 0.0088 highest in Feb), LuxFitting "Friday Tips" (13 Mar: best of March) — specific framing beats generic labels.
- Generic "Customer" emails need either exceptional segmentation or a specific subject line to compete. When they win, it is because the underlying list segment is excellent — not because the content strategy is sound.

### 3 Things to STOP DOING

**1. Sending +Yahoo segment blasts outside major, proven sale moments**
- The data is conclusive: every +Yahoo send in the dataset lands in the bottom 1–2 sends of its month by CBH/Del.
- SantaFare Mar 26 (+Yahoo): 0.0003 CBH/Del. SantaFare Feb 26 (+Yahoo): 0.0012. These are not edge cases — they are the predictable outcome of appending an inactive, non-MPP list to a struggling send.
- If +Yahoo must be used, gate it to confirmed high-revenue sale events (Black Friday, Valentine's Day peak) where the offer itself can overcome list quality. Do not use for Easter, Year End, or generic monthly "Final" sends.

**2. Sending two emails of the same type within two weeks to the same audience**
- SantaFare sent birthday emails on both 05 Mar (0.0050 CBH/Del) and 19 Mar (0.0008 CBH/Del) — the second send produced 84% less revenue per delivered email.
- GentsLux sent "Winter 2025" twice in November (19 Nov: 0.0131, 26 Nov: 0.0053) — the repeat halved CBH/Del.
- LuxFitting Feb 06 Happy Birthday (0.0046) and Feb 20 Happy Birthday (0.0057) — two birthday sends in 14 days, both underperform. The second is only marginally better because the list may differ slightly.
- The audience's "hiring" decision for a given email type only happens once per occasion. Repeat sends signal desperation rather than relevance.

**3. "Year End Review" / "Year in Review" content at any cadence**
- Dec 31 GentsLux "Year in Review": 0.0041 CBH/Del, 0.0079 Access/Open — worst in December.
